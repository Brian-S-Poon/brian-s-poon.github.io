About Myself

Jocelyn Nguyen is a multidisciplinary artist and vocalist whose work lives at the intersection of memory, melancholy, and distortion. As a member of Vox Obscura, she shapes sound into stories—layering ethereal vocals, minimalist production, and surreal textures to evoke a world that feels both intimate and untouchable.

With roots in classical piano and spoken word, Jocelyn’s sonic evolution began in dim bedrooms and late-night loops. Her music leans into stillness as much as chaos—often exploring themes of identity, dream logic, and the quiet violence of time. Field recordings, processed voice fragments, and analog synths frequently weave through her compositions, giving her tracks a cinematic, ghostlike presence.

Her debut release with Vox Obscura, Glass Rituals, introduced listeners to a haunting new voice in the experimental landscape—one that doesn’t demand to be heard, but stays with you long after the sound fades. Whether collaborating within the collective or performing in immersive, audio-visual spaces, Jocelyn continues to expand what it means to be vulnerable in a distorted world.
